Anantapur District Tourism

Explore Anantapur—from grand forts and ancient temples to the world’s largest banyan and serene spiritual spots.

Gooty Fort

Shell-shaped hill fort from the Vijayanagara era, with 15 gates, panoramic views, and ancient inscriptions.

Travel Tips:
  • Distance: ~52 km from Anantapur
  • Best Time: Oct–Feb
  • Activities: Heritage walk, photography
Lepakshi – Veerabhadra Temple

A Vijayanagara marvel featuring intricately carved pillars, vivid murals, hanging pillar, and a majestic Nandi statue.

Travel Tips:
  • Distance: ~110 km from Anantapur
  • Best Time: Year-round
  • Activities: Art, architecture, heritage exploration
Thimmamma Marrimanu

The world’s largest banyan tree, sprawling across 5 acres and revered as a blessing spot for childless couples.

Travel Tips:
  • Distance: ~35 km from Anantapur
  • Best Time: Year-round
  • Activities: Spiritual reverence, picnics
Bugga Ramalingeswara Swamy Temple

Vijayanagara-era Shiva shrine in Tadipatri with musical pillars producing ‘saptaswara’ and an unfinished gopuram lauded as a marvel.

Travel Tips:
  • Location: Tadipatri (~52 km)
  • Built: 1490–1509
  • Activities: Architecture, acoustic marvels
Penna Ahobilam – Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Temple

Perched on a hill, this temple houses divine footprints of Lord Narasimha and hosts an elaborate chariot festival.

Travel Tips:
  • Distance: ~41 km from Anantapur
  • Festivals: April–May
  • Activities: Pilgrimage, holy festivals
Anantapur Clock Tower

A civic heritage landmark inaugurated on August 15, 1947, with symbolism in every dimension reflecting independence.

Travel Tips:
  • Location: Anantapur city center
  • Symbols: August 1947
  • Activities: City stroll, history appreciation
