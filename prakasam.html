Prakasam District Tourism

Discover Prakasam: from the tranquil sands of Ramapuram & Kothapatnam Beaches to the mystical cave temples of Bhairavakona and UNESCO-tagged Cumbum Tank.

Bhairavakona Cave Temples

Rock-cut Shiva temples (8 in number) carved into the heart of the Nallamala Hills, dating back to the 7th–8th century CE.

Travel Tips:
  • Location: Nallamala Hills, Prakasam District
  • Best Time: October–March (clear weather, moonlight view during Kartika Purnima)
  • Activities: Heritage exploration, nature walks, witnessing moonlit idols
Ramapuram Beach

The cleanest, most peaceful beach in Prakasam—leisurely sunrises, seafood shacks on the sand, and barely a crowd.

Travel Tips:
  • Nearest Town: Chirala
  • Best Time: October–March, and during early morning and evening
  • Activities: Beach walks, sunsets/sunrises, local seafood tasting
Kothapatnam Beach

A serene coastal village known for its long sandy shores, clear waters, and gentle sea breeze—great for boating.

Travel Tips:
  • Location: ~18 km from Ongole
  • Best Time: October–March
  • Activities: Boating, photography, beachside relaxation
Pakala Beach

Quiet beach in Pakala, with clean sands and mesmerizing clear waters. Boating available for added fun.

Travel Tips:
  • Location: Pakala village, near Singarayakonda
  • Best Time: October–March
  • Activities: Boating, peaceful retreat, scenic views
Cumbum Tank

One of Asia’s oldest man-made lakes—built by Vijayanagara Princess Ruchidevi—now holds the prestigious UNESCO WHIS tag.

Travel Tips:
  • Location: Cumbum area, Prakasam District
  • Best Time: Post-monsoon to winter (October–February)
  • Activities: Nature walks, heritage appreciation, photography
Tripuranthakeswara Temple

Hill-top Shiva temple at Tripuranthakam, paired with Sri Bala Tripurasundari shrine below—an evocative blend of devotion and drama.

Travel Tips:
  • Location: Tripuranthakam, on the Guntur–Srisailam highway
  • Best Time: Year-round
  • Activities: Pilgrimage, panoramic views, spiritual ambiance
