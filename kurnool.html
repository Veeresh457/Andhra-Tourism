Kurnool District Tourism

From ancient forts and sacred shrines to mysterious caves and sculpted rock gardens—Kurnool invites you to explore its heritage and nature.

Belum Caves

India’s second-longest natural cave system with fascinating stalactite and stalagmite formations.

Travel Tips:
  • Best Time: September–February
  • Activities: Cave exploration, geology photography
Konda Reddy Fort

A 16th-century fortification named after its legendary occupant, offering panoramic city views.

Travel Tips:
  • Location: Heart of Kurnool city
  • Activities: History, lookout views, photography
Sri Yaganti Temple

Home to a rare Ardhanareeswara idol (half Shiva, half Parvati) carved from a single rock, located amid scenic hills.

Travel Tips:
  • Best Time: Maha Shivaratri (Oct–Nov)
  • Activities: Pilgrimage, cave vistas, spiritual solace
Rollapadu Wildlife Sanctuary

Grassland sanctuary home to rare species like the Great Indian Bustard, wolves, and floricans.

Travel Tips:
  • Best Time: August–December
  • Activities: Birdwatching, wildlife trails, photography
Mahanandi Temple

A 7th-century temple in the Nallamala Hills, famed for its one of nine sacred Nandi shrines and sacred water tanks.

Travel Tips:
  • Best Time: Year-round
  • Activities: Pilgrimage, ritual baths, architecture
