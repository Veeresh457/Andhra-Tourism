Guntur District Tourism

Guntur District, famous for Amaravati Stupa, ancient temples, Kondaveedu Fort, and Mangalagiri, is a blend of history, culture, and spirituality.

Amaravati Stupa

An ancient Buddhist stupa, symbolizing peace and spiritual heritage, located on the banks of Krishna River.

Travel Tips:
  • Nearest City: Amaravati
  • Best Time: October–March
  • Activities: Spiritual visit, heritage walk, photography
Kondaveedu Fort

A historical hill fort with 21 structures, offering scenic views and a glimpse into Andhra’s medieval past.

Travel Tips:
  • Nearest Town: Kondaveedu (25 km from Guntur)
  • Best Time: October–February
  • Activities: Trekking, heritage exploration, photography
Mangalagiri Temple

One of the eight important sacred places of Lord Narasimha, known for its unique Panakala Narasimha Swamy temple.

Travel Tips:
  • Nearest City: Guntur/Vijayawada
  • Best Time: Year-round
  • Activities: Pilgrimage, rituals, sightseeing
Uppalapadu Bird Sanctuary

A serene sanctuary home to thousands of migratory birds including pelicans and painted storks.

Travel Tips:
  • Nearest City: Guntur
  • Best Time: November–February
  • Activities: Birdwatching, nature photography
Amaralingeswara Temple

A sacred temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, one of the Pancharama Kshetras in Andhra Pradesh.

Travel Tips:
  • Nearest City: Amaravati
  • Best Time: Year-round
  • Activities: Pilgrimage, rituals, spiritual learning
Ethipothala Waterfalls

A stunning 70 feet high waterfall on Chandravanka River, surrounded by lush greenery and caves.

Travel Tips:
  • Nearest Town: Nagarjuna Sagar (close to Guntur border)
  • Best Time: August–December
  • Activities: Trekking, nature walk, photography
