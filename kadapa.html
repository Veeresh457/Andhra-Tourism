Kadapa District Tourism

Discover Kadapa—gateway to heritage forts, sacred temples, scenic canyons, and ancient caves wrapped in spiritual mystique.

Gandikota Fort (Gandikota Canyon)

13th-century fortress overlooking the awe-inspiring Pennar river gorge, often called the 'Grand Canyon of India'.

Travel Tips:
  • Best Time: Winter (Nov–Feb)
  • Activities: Heritage walk, canyon views, camping, sunrise & sunset views
Pushpagiri Temple Complex

A sacred temple cluster dating back to the 7th century CE, rich with mythological significance and architectural detail.

Travel Tips:
  • Activities: Pilgrimage, exploring temple architecture
Kodandarama Temple, Vontimitta

Majestic 16th-century Dravidian-style temple built by Chola and Vijayanagara kings with exquisite carvings.

Travel Tips:
  • Location: ~25 km from Kadapa
  • Activities: Pilgrimage, architectural photography
Belum Caves

The longest natural caves in India, stretching over 3,229 meters with subterranean depths and rock formations.

Travel Tips:
  • Best Time: Post-monsoon to winter
  • Activities: Cave exploration, lighting photography
Bhagwan Mahavir Government Museum

Archaeological museum preserving artifacts from 5th to 18th century excavated across Kadapa region.

Travel Tips:
  • Best Time: Year-round
  • Activities: History, archaeology, guided tours
Gandi Veeranjaneya Temple

Ancient temple where legend states Rama originally drew Hanuman’s image on rock—Veeranjaneya resides here.

Travel Tips:
  • Location: On banks of Papaghni River, ~34 km from Pulivendula
  • Activities: Pilgrimage, local folklore, scenic backdrop
